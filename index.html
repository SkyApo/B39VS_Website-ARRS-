Who We Are

ABOUT BLUE­ENGINEERING

BlueEngineering is a student-led engineering team formed by a group of passionate individuals who wanted to go beyond lectures, tutorials, and exams. What began as a small collaboration between classmates has grown into a platform where we actively design, build, and test ideas that aim to solve real-world problems.

As engineering students, we realised early on that true understanding comes from application. BlueEngineering was created to bridge that gap — giving us a space to experiment, make mistakes, iterate, and ultimately turn concepts into working solutions. Through this, we develop not only technical skills, but also our ability to think critically, adapt, and work effectively as a team.

Our work focuses on automation, smart systems, and digital integration. We are particularly interested in emerging technologies like IoT, AI, and data-driven engineering, and how these can be applied in practical, meaningful ways. Rather than building for the sake of complexity, we aim to create solutions that are efficient, purposeful, and grounded in real use cases.

We may still be students, but we are driven by a genuine desire to create, contribute, and continuously improve. BlueEngineering represents that mindset — a starting point for ideas that we hope will one day scale beyond the classroom.

PROJECT PROFILES

ARRS
Autonomous Robot Recycling System

ARRS is BlueEngineering's autonomous mobile robot platform designed to address the challenge of recyclable material sorting and handling. The system integrates a mecanum-wheel chassis for omnidirectional mobility with a multi-axis robotic arm capable of identifying, gripping, and sorting recyclable items autonomously.

Designed and assembled entirely in-house by the team, the ARRS hardware architecture features onboard processing, sensor fusion, and a rotating turntable mechanism for efficient material management — a practical prototype for smart recycling automation.

  • Omnidirectional mecanum-wheel drive for precise, unrestricted movement
  • Multi-axis robotic arm with end-effector gripper for material handling
  • Onboard sensors for environmental awareness and obstacle avoidance
  • Rotating turntable mechanism for sorting zone management
  • Integrated electronics bay with motor controllers and processing unit
  • LCD status display and emergency stop for safe operation
  • Compact, modular chassis designed for iterative improvement
  • Fully designed and assembled in-house by the BlueEngineering team

IVIS
Integrated Vision Inspection System

IVIS is a wearable AI-powered defect detection system designed to transform Quality Assurance and Quality Control (QAQC) in the construction industry. It addresses two critical pain points: ineffective defect reporting during Material Receiving Inspection (MRI) and heavy reliance on error-prone manual labour during Work Inspection Request (WIR) stages.

Built around safety goggles with dual ESP32-CAM modules, IVIS provides hands-free, real-time defect detection — automatically logging findings via GPS, converting coordinates to project grid references, and integrating directly with BIM models and IFC drawings for precise defect localisation.

  • GPS-tagged inspection points converted to project grid coordinates
  • Automatic cloud logging and comprehensive defect report generation
  • Direct integration into IFC drawings and BIM models
  • Reduces construction waste from 54.6 t/100m² to ~1–1.5 t
  • Extendable to automotive, drone inspection, and solar panel monitoring
  • Compliant with MCMC, ISO 19650, and DOSH safety guidelines
